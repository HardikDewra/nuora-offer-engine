Strategic Test Recommendations
Prioritized recommendations based on data patterns from 8 A/B tests
1
Test Individual Elements on Winning Control
Every variant tested so far changed 5+ elements simultaneously (layout, naming, pricing, toggle, CTA). This makes it impossible to isolate what specifically hurts performance. Instead, take the winning control and change ONE element at a time.

First test: Keep everything identical to control but change CTA from "ADD TO CART" to "GET STARTED". This isolates CTA copy impact with zero confounding variables.
HIGH IMPACT LOW RISK
2
Test Savings Display on Control Layout
The control shows no savings information on pack cards. Test adding "Save $X" or "X% OFF" badges to the existing control horizontal cards while keeping everything else identical. This tests whether savings visibility helps or hurts when the rest of the UI is already optimized.
MEDIUM IMPACT LOW RISK
3
Test Per-Day Pricing on Control
Control doesn't show per-day or per-serving cost. Several variants included this (Omre: "$1.33/day", Ryze: "$0.59/serving"). Test adding per-day cost to the existing control cards. At $1.14/day for the 3-pack, this could anchor value perception without changing layout.
MEDIUM IMPACT LOW RISK
4
Test Urgency/Countdown in Offer Module
Control has countdown timer in header only. Ryze variants placed "Sale Ends in" countdown directly inside the offer module. Test moving the countdown timer closer to the CTA button within the existing control layout. Proximity to decision point may increase urgency.
MEDIUM IMPACT LOW RISK
5
Test "Most Popular" Badge on 3-Pack
Control has no badges on pack cards. Multiple variants used "Best Deal" or "Most Popular" badges. Test adding a subtle "Most Popular" indicator on the 3-pack option in the control layout. Social proof on the highest-value option may nudge bundle size up.
MEDIUM IMPACT LOW RISK
6
Test Dynamic CTA with Price
Control shows static "ADD TO CART". Ankhway V2 showed "BUY NOW | ~~$150~~ $99.99". Test making the control's CTA dynamic: "ADD TO CART - $58.99" showing the selected price. Reinforces value at the action point.
MEDIUM IMPACT LOW RISK
7
Fix Accidental Subscription Issue
33.2% of subscription cancellations cite "I subscribed by accident". This is the single largest churn driver. While not an offer test, improving the subscription checkbox clarity (perhaps adding a brief tooltip or confirmation step) could reduce cancellations by up to 33% - a massive revenue retention opportunity.
CRITICAL HIGH IMPACT
8
Avoid These Patterns in Future Tests
Based on 8 tests, these patterns consistently underperform:
- Vertical stacked cards (avg -1.8% conversion)
- Side-by-side Sub/OTP toggle (avg -2.2% conversion)
- BOGO naming "Buy X Get Y Free" (eliminates OTP entirely, -1.7% conversion)
- Subscription-only model (loses 10-30% of potential orders)
- Removing or reducing the 2-pack middle option
AVOID
Recommended Testing Methodology
How to run the next round of tests effectively

One Variable at a Time

Change exactly ONE element per test. This isolates causation. All 8 previous tests changed 5+ variables, making it impossible to identify the specific driver of performance differences.

Minimum 2,000 Visitors

Tests with fewer than 1,500 visitors (like Resilia at 1,004) have insufficient statistical power. Target 2,000+ visitors per test for reliable results. At current traffic, this means 12-15 hours runtime.

Always Run Against Live Control

The control's conversion rate varies (5.94% to 8.99%) due to traffic quality fluctuations. Always run the control simultaneously - never compare against historical control numbers.

Track Subscription Rate Separately

A variant could match control on conversion but destroy subscription rate. Always track the Sub/OTP split as a primary metric alongside conversion rate. For Nuora's model, subscription rate is just as important as overall conversion.
